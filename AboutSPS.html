About The Society of Physics Students

The Society of Physics Students (SPS) has more than 6,000 members in nearly 700 colleges and universities. An active SPS chapter provides the professional development opportunities that course work cannot teach, including development of communication and professional networking skills, undergraduate research and science literacy outreach to the community.

The SPS chapter can also be a physics department's most effective recruitment and retention tool. An active chapter helps to transform students from being mere class attendees to being contributing members of a professional community. And it certainly does not require its members to be physics students! Anyone interested in physics or its applications can be a member of both the national organization and the local chapter.

The Society of Physics Students at Berea College is the 508th chapter of SPS. We believe SPS has great effects on communications between undergraduate physics students at Hopkins. Not only is SPS responsible for the information exchange and link between the faculty and the students, but it also serves as a social environment for undergraduate physics majors.

Being a member at Berea College means coming to the meetings and being on the Berea College Outlook Group mailing list. To join the national SPS, you must send $20 and an information form to the national headquarters. For more information, go here. To join the national organization, go here

If you enjoy hearing about current research in physics, helping others to understand physics and having fun, then you should consider being a member of the Society of Physics Students at Berea College. We hope to see you at our meetings!

Berea College Society of Physics Students Constitution

Preamble

We—the physics enthusiasts of Berea College—believe in exploring the natural sciences, physics in particular, as well as garnering an interest in these in our community through events, activities, and healthy competitions. As proponents of the eight Great Commitments of Berea College, we work tirelessly to uphold them and the central doctrine of a healthy and active lifestyle, with an emphasis on science education.

ARTICLE I

NAME, PURPOSE, OBJECTIVE

Section 1.1 (Name)

The name of this club shall be the Berea College Society of Physics Students (BC SPS).

Section 1.2 (Purpose)

The purpose of the SPS is to expose students to science concepts and open ways to experiment and examine these concepts safely and creatively with peers. Furthermore, promotion of science literacy and outreach in the community (i.e. Berea Community School) is a priority.

Section 1.3 (Objective)
  1. The SPS shall strive to promote an attitude of inclusion in a habitat of experiential learning, offering an environment for personal, group, and leadership growth through natural science adventures.
  2. All members shall have access to skill-appropriate experiences in which participant and facilitator safety is placed before any other goal, in which the decision not to participate in any activity will have no impact upon a participant’s membership in the club.
  3. All members shall be instructed to observe and obey proper safety measures, as dictated by the supervising officers.
  4. All members shall accept the responsibility of promoting an attitude of inclusion for all, and an understanding of the negative impacts of ostracizing others, as well as the SPS’s stance on the matter.
  5. Increase knowledge of club members about natural phenomena in our world.
  6. Increase the quality of the college experience for students through group growth and challenge.

ARTICLE II

MEMBERSHIP, FEES, OFFICERS

Section 2.1 (Membership)
  1. All Berea College Students meeting the membership guidelines are welcome to join the SPS.
  2. Club members must:
    • Be currently enrolled to Berea College on a full-time basis; and
    • Agree to follow the guidelines included in this Constitution; and
    • Must not be on labor, or social probation.
  3. Any Berea College student wishing to be a member should request permission verbally or in writing to any member of the SPS officers. Upon review of the above standards, membership may be granted.
Section 2.2 (Fees)
  1. The SPS holds that no student should be turned away on the basis of financial inability.
Section 2.3 (Officers)
  1. Officers will be a Coordinator, Event Planner, Resource Manager, Promotions Officer, and Documentation Officer.
  2. Officers will be elected on the basis of nomination, and will be appointed through a secret ballot conducted by all present club members. Club elections will be held no later than the 4th week of each academic semester.
  3. Duties of the Officers:

Coordinator – Max Two Terms

  1. Presides at all meetings and assumes the responsibility for administration of actions voted by the Executive Board.
  2. Oversees the financial situation of the club with VP of Finance and Logistics to support operation of programs, activities and events.
  3. Carry out the provisions of the constitution.
  4. Be willing to lead 2/3 of all SPS activities per semester.
  5. Attend 2/3 of all SPS activities per semester.
  6. Plans and oversights the programs, after programs are approved by the Executive Board.
  7. Responsible to ensure collaboration of SPS with other colleges and on-campus organizations.

Event Planner

  1. Assume the duties of the Coordinator in his/her absence.
  2. Brainstorms ideas for programs and activities which promote education and understanding.
  3. Perform any duties delegated by the Coordinator.
  4. Creates timeline of programs and activities for each semester.
  5. Be willing to lead 2/3 of all SPS activities per semester.
  6. Attend 2/3 of all SPS activities per semester.
  7. Oversees and encourages all programs and activities organized or co-organized by the club.
  8. Works with other clubs and organizations to co-organize programs and activities.

Promotions Officer

  1. Oversees marketing and promotions of programs and activities such as distributing flyers and posters and scheduling dining services tabling along with updating SPS members.
  2. Get all information required for the flyers, posters, brochures, etc. of all programs and activities.
  3. Designs flyers, posters, brochures, etc. for all programs and activities of the club at least one week before the date of event.
  4. Represent the SPS through regular articles in the campus newspaper.
  5. Manages club’s social media channels (currently Facebook account and page) and B-Linked.

Resource Manager

  1. Manage SPS finances.
  2. Receive all funds and process Request for Payment, Deposit Slip, and Signature Forms.
  3. Keep financial records and collect dues (when necessary). Manages human resource - recruit members, record payment of membership dues, update membership databases.
  4. Pay bills and release funds as voted by the Executive Council.
  5. Communicate and coordinate with faculty advisor regularly.
  6. Oversees financial situation of the club with the Coordinator to support smooth execution of programs and activities - create budget for each program or activity, and organize fundraisers.
  7. Responsible to ensure venue and food for programs and activities.

Documentation Officer

  1. Responsible for documenting club events, via blog/social media.
  2. Responsible for photography of all events for social media/blog posts.
  3. Expected to be present at all/most meetings.
  4. Records meeting agendas and minutes.
  5. Record and keep accurate minutes of all meetings.
  6. Maintain a current roster of membership.

ARTICLE III

Executive Council

Section 3.1 (Council)
  1. The Executive Council will consist of the Coordinator, Event Planner, Resource Manager, Promotions Officer, and Documentation Officer, who shall meet as often as necessary.
  2. Duties of the Executive Council
    1. To formulate policy of the SPS between regular meetings and in case of emergencies. Special cases are subject to approval by the faculty advisor.
    2. To govern activities of the club, as outlined in section 2.3.4.
    3. To compile calendar for semester activities.
    4. To represent Berea College in a responsible fashion.
    5. To keep open communication between the club activities or governances and the faculty advisor.

ARTICLE IV

IMPEACHMENT AND REPLACEMENT OF MEMBERS, OFFICERS

All elected officers and club members may be subjected to impeachment and removal by a two-thirds majority vote of the Executive Council.

Section 4.1 (Impeachment)
  1. Grounds for impeachment are negligence and any form of misconduct which is damaging to the club. An officer/member may be impeached only under the following conditions:
    1. There must be a quorum present during impeachment (a quorum is 50 percent plus 1 of the current board members); and
    2. A majority vote must be reached; and
    3. Prior to impeachment, there must be one week's notice of intent publicized.
  2. In the case of any club member or officer who elicits probable cause for threat to the physical safety of any person, the Executive Council may hold a vote in which a two-thirds majority decides upon impeachment.
  3. Any officer vacancies shall be filled by an election held within two weeks.

ARTICLE V

Advisor

Section 5.1 (Advisor)

According to the Berea College Club and Organization Handbook, “every recognized organization is required to have an advisor. He/she must be a full-time member of the faculty or professional, administrative staff. This advisor shall be kept abreast of all activities and business unique to the organization. The advisor should also be consulted on matters of Berea College policy and procedures. Faculty/Staff advisors or a chosen representative must be present as representatives of Berea College for all official organization events on and off campus” (6).
